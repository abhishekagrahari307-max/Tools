Physics - Class 12: Electrostatics

Question 1 of 20 14:35 Score: 0

Two point charges +q and -q are placed at a distance d apart. What is the electric field at a point on the perpendicular bisector of the line joining the charges?

Solution

The correct answer is D: Proportional to q/d.

For a dipole, the electric field on the perpendicular bisector (equatorial line) is given by:

E = kp/r where p = qd is the dipole moment

At a distance r from the center, the field is:

E = kqd/((r + (d/2))

For r >> d, E kqd/r

This shows the field is proportional to q and inversely proportional to d.
